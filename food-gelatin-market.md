<p><p>The food gelatin market comprises several types, including skin gelatin, bone gelatin, and halal gelatin. Skin gelatin is derived from the hides of animals and is commonly used in confectionery and desserts for its gelling properties. Bone gelatin, extracted from animal bones, is often utilized in broths, soups, and jellies. Halal gelatin is produced from animals slaughtered according to Islamic law, catering to Muslim consumers. Each type serves distinct applications in the food industry, ensuring diverse culinary uses.</p></p>
