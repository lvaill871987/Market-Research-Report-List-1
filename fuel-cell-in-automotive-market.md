<p><ul><li>PEMFC (Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells)</li><li>MCFC (Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells)</li><li>SOFC (Solid Oxide Fuel Cells)</li><li>PAFC (Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>In the automotive market, fuel cells are categorized into several types based on their operational principles. Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells (PEMFC) are widely used for their efficiency and quick start-up, suitable for light vehicles. Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells (MCFC) operate at high temperatures and are used in larger applications. Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC) are also high-temperature systems, ideal for heavy-duty vehicles. Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells (PAFC) are stable and robust but less common. Other emerging technologies continue to diversify the market.</p></p>
